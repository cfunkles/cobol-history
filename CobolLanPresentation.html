COBOL

Common Business Oriented Language

COBOL is a high-level programming language for business applications developed in 1959 by CODASYL (Conference/Committe on Data Systems Languages). COBOL is based off of Grace Hopper's Flow-Matic programming language. COBOL was concieved at the Pentagon.

What is the purpose of COBOL?

COBOL is a language many businesses use to work with large amounts of data, such as financial transactions. COBOL is a high level language that is written almost like English. An example of COBOL would be Add $ to Account, whereas in Javascript you would write balance += paycheck;. The CODASYL committee that conjured COBOL wanted the language to be easy to use and accessable to many programers. COBOL is really good at handeling batch data.

COBAL is divided into four divisions

  • Environment Division
  • Data Division
  • Procedure Division

A timeline history of COBOL

  • 1955-1959 Grace Hopper developed the Flow-Matic compiler language to express business data operations more like English statements.
  • May 1959 COBOL was born in a meeting with 41 people include Grace Hopper at the Pentagon. After agreeing that more people should be able to program utilizing COBOL's English like syntax.
  • June 1959 The committee responsible for the development of COBOL was named CODASYL.
    The commitee consisted of 3 goverment agencies-
    1. National Bureau of Standards
    2. Department of Defence
    3. Navy's David Taylor Model Basin
    and 6 computer manufacturers-
    1. Burroughs Corporation
    2. IBM
    3. Minneapolis-Honeywell
    4. RCA
    5. Sperry Rand
    6. Sylvania Electric Products
  • January 1960 COBOL-60 was approved and new manufacturing companies started using COBOL on their systems.
  • 1961 COBOL-61 was released. The first versions of COBOL could only do 3 to 11 statements per minute. Newer versions of COBOL were released with improved compilers, and therefore computers needed more memory to perform at faster speeds.
  • 1965 COBOL-65 was released bringing more clarifications and with ability to control mass storage files and tables better.
  • 1968 COBOL-68 released to overcome incompatibility issues.
  • 1974 COBOL-74 was released and becomes the most widely used programming language on the planet. The COBOL-74 version had new features like delete, examine, and ispect.
  • 1985 COBOL-85 was released with new features, deleted features, and revised features.
  • 1997 COBOL now has 200 billion lies of code used in 80% of businesses. Developers start to worry about Y2K! Work starts on fixing date issues. Many programmers come out of retirement to solve these issues.
